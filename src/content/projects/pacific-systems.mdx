## Brief

Pacific Systems starts from a simple premise: regional identity can be quiet, structured, and modern. The goal was to build a visual system that could move between event posters, social campaigns, printed programs, and a digital archive without losing its voice.

## Concept

The system uses editorial typography, low-contrast texture, and a restrained palette to create a brand language that feels grounded rather than decorative. Layouts borrow from field notes, museum labels, and product catalogs.

## Design System

The identity is built around three behaviors:

- Large serif headlines that act as visual anchors
- Monospaced metadata for place, date, role, and programming details
- Image crops that move between atmosphere and detail

## Outcome

The final direction creates a brand platform that can support cultural programming, project documentation, and campaign work with the same underlying visual grammar.
