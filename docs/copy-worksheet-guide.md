# Copy Worksheet Guide

Use `copy-worksheet.csv` to give rough answers for the site copy. You do not need to write finished copy.

## How to fill it

- Start with all `P1` rows. These are enough for a strong first rewrite.
- Fill `your_answer` in plain language. Notes, fragments, and bullet-like phrases are fine.
- Leave `current_draft` untouched so we can compare the old copy to the new direction.
- Use `example_answer` only as a model for the kind of detail that helps.
- `P2` rows improve captions, supporting sections, and page polish.
- `P3` rows are mostly accessibility text and can wait until the final images are chosen.

## What I will do with it

After you fill in the CSV, I will interpret your answers and rewrite the site copy directly in the matching files listed under `source_file`. The goal is launch-ready copy that sounds confident, specific, and natural for a graphic design portfolio.
